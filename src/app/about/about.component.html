Welcome

I am Swapnil

I come from Mymensingh, a city in the northern part of Bangladesh. As a student, I've always been intrigued by math and problem-solving. During high school, I participated in competitions like math Olympiads and explored various scientific concepts, including philosophy.

This sense of curiosity has always driven me to explore the unknown. When it came time to choose a major, many interests pulled me in different directions, but my passion for problem-solving led me to pursue computer science.

In late 2019, I began my bachelor’s degree in computer science. As a first-year student, I won the university programming contest in the junior category. Since then, I've consistently ranked well in university programming contests, which allowed me to participate in national competitions like IUPC and NCPC, as well as international contests like ICPC.

During my senior year, I rediscovered my love for exploration, which drew me towards research. Since then, I have conducted multiple research projects in Natural Language Processing, Computer Vision, and Bioinformatics. I graduated in December 2023 and joined Enosis as a software engineer.

In my career, I've had the privilege of learning from extraordinary mentors, initially focusing on engineering principles and clean code. Over time, I’ve mastered complex engineering concepts like design patterns and architectural patterns, consistently applying them to my work.

Though I initially lost touch with research during the first six months of my job, the explorer within me never faded. Now, I’m continuing my research, particularly in LLMs and AI generation. I see a close connection between engineering, research, and problem-solving, and I am passionate about solving challenges in both fields. My story is still unfolding, and I hope to achieve great things in the future. Thanks for reading, and please keep me in your prayers.
